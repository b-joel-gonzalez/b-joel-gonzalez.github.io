Research

As a graduate student, I've become very interested in hardware development, particularly within electronics engineering. I want to work to develop more efficient electronics, which push the boundaries of today's computational power. I'm interested in working across all disciplines of electronics, from digital to analog to power, and utilizing a multitude of technologies, from mm-wave to RF and more.

To this end, I've chosen to focus on an electronics project for my master's thesis; I seek to develop a hybrid sensor device for occupancy detection. I'm looking into a wide variety of sensors - from mm-wave to PIR to image - to see how I might combine them in order to develop an efficient, novel device that accurately detects a human occupant. Ultimately, I aim to manufacture a PCB prototype as a proof-of-concept.

For my master's thesis, I'm working under the guidance of Dr. Camillo Jose Taylor (of GRASP at UPenn) and Dr. Madhu Annapragada along with two companies at Pennovation - Instahub and Automation Research Group (the latter of which Dr. Annapragada owns). I will link my thesis here once it's completed in May 2021! Once I finish my master's degree, I hope to eventually pursue a Ph.D. in electrical engineering in order to conduct novel research and to help expand the field of electronics.
